The Geometry course fosters spatial and logical reasoning in the plane and space as it engages students in the study of concepts such as parallelism, perpendicularity, similarity, congruence, transformations, and the measurement of angles, segments, polygons, and circles. Students develop skills in formulating proof, calculating surface area and volume of solids, working with coordinate geometry, and investigating basic trigonometric properties.

Essential Questions and/or Primary Course Objectives:

What is Geometry?

  1. How are Geometry concepts usefully applied in today's world?

  2. How does using formal proof develop logic and reasoning skills?

  3. How can we calculate/measure the relationships and properties of shapes and solids?
